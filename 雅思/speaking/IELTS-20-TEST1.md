# IELTS-20-TEST1

## PART1
- **How much walking do you do in your daily life?**
```
I don't really track how much walking I do daily. My routine is quit simple -- I walk about 10 minutes to the subway 
station, then head to work. However, I'm a bit overweight and have high blood pressure. My docter suggests I exercise 
regularly. So I walk twice a week for about 30 minutes each time. That's how I balance walking in my daily life.
```

- **Did you walk more when you were at school than now?**
```
I definitely walked more when I was at school than now. Back then, I often hung out with friends doing outdoor activities. 
In summer vacations, we'd go fishing, running to different lakes. It's a wonderful memory. But now, at almost 40, as I 
said, my daily life is simple -- just walking to work and home. I occasionally do some exercise or activities now. So, 
yes, I walked more in school than I do now.
```

- **What places are there to go for a walk near where you live?**
```
This is a school near where I live. I used to reserve the playground from the school app, but sometimes it's unavailable 
due to events. Now I prefer walking around the school. The area is open, and many people do similar activities. Outside, 
there are elderly dancing, and it feels livelier. In contrast, the playground quickly becomes boring with less fresh air. 
So I feel more comfortable, especially when a breeze cools me down on a hot day. So, walking outside is better for me.
```

- **Would you ever like to go on a walking holiday?**
```
Yes, I would like to go on a walking holidy. As I said, my daily life is quite monotonous, mainly working or studying 
at weekend. After all, sometimes I need a break, I think a walking holiday is good option. For example, a coastal walkway 
allows me to see the vast sea, hear the breeze, and feel calm. The comfortable temperature and open view help feel relaxed.
That's why I'd choose a walking holiday.
```
